What FleetWatch Does

Clear oversight of onboard system health

FleetWatch monitors the health and stability of onboard computers and critical applications so operators can see when systems are drifting, slowing down, or failing.

It is built for vessel operators, superintendents, and support teams who need dependable information without extra noise. The focus is simple: keep key systems available, stable, and ready for daily work.

What We Monitor

Three areas that matter onboard

A
Stability

System Stability

Core operating conditions that affect whether onboard computers stay dependable.

  • Service crashes
  • Unexpected reboots
  • Boot failures
  • Driver and hardware issues
  • Disk or memory problems
  • Overheating or throttling
B
Performance

Performance & Uptime

Practical indicators that show when systems are slowing down or dropping out.

  • Resource exhaustion (CPU, RAM, disk)
  • Systems going offline
  • Connectivity gaps
  • Log pipeline interruptions during satellite outages
C
Application Health

Application-Level Health

Stability checks for the applications crews and support teams rely on every day.

  • Planned Maintenance System errors (database, sync, service failures)
  • Charting and navigation software issues (chart update failures, licensing, driver problems)
  • Email system issues (SMTP/IMAP failures, queue buildup, DNS problems)

Real-world example

Operator scenario

Spam Flood on a Vessel

A vessel suddenly receives hundreds of spam emails per minute, slowing down the mail client and congesting the satellite link. FleetWatch detects the abnormal volume, extracts the relevant log entries, and alerts your support team with everything needed to submit to your email provider.

For operators who prefer a hands-off approach, our team can manage the full end-to-end remediation or incident response coordination with third-party vendors, from sample extraction to provider submission, ensuring the vessel returns to normal operation quickly.
